Dry orgasm in men

Dry orgasm is characterized by the absence of seminal fluid during orgasm, scientifically called orgasmic anejaculation. This is a form of ejaculation - a condition in which men cannot ejaculate fluid, even if they have a normal erection.

Depending on the cause, dry orgasms can be temporary or permanent.

What are the causes of orgasmic anejaculation?

Most often, it occurs after surgery on the bladder or prostate, when the muscles and nerves that play a role in the blood flow are damaged. ejaculations. For example, after a radical prostatectomy due to prostate cancer, there is a constant dry orgasm.

Other causes of dry orgasm are:

- multiple orgasms: getting multiple orgasms in a short period in young men can cause dry orgasms, as the body needs to time for the production of ejaculate;

- nerve disorders in diabetes, multiple sclerosis, spinal injuries;

- obstruction of the VAS deferens;

- low level of male hormone;

- taking certain medications;

- radiation treatment in the pelvis.

Stressful and other situations psychological reasons can also play a role, but they are temporary.

Dry orgasm should be distinguished from retrograde ejaculation. In this disease, the function of the sphincter of the neck of the bladder is disrupted, and it remains open during ejaculation. Semen from the urethra enters the urinary tract the bladder, and as a result, no ejaculate is released during orgasm. But when urinating after sex, you can notice that the urine is more cloudy, because it contains liquid.

What are the consequences of dry orgasms?

Since most men experience orgasms, the lack of ejaculation is often not for them problem. However, dry orgasms can cause a number of psychological problems for some men. In particular, it can cause a sense of discomfort and shame, make him refrain from sexual intercourse, lead to a decrease in the male sexual organ. It can also affect a man's reproductive ability. Although the conception under such circumstances, it is not possible, there are other alternatives for extracting seminal fluid and artificial insemination.

How is dry orgasm diagnosed?

For the diagnosis of dry orgasm, it is important to have a medical history of taking medications, performing surgery, and physical examination of the male sexual organ, testicles, rectum. Urine is tested for the presence of semen after orgasm.

Additional examinations are needed to determine the cause of a lack of sperm during orgasm.

How to treat dry orgasm?

Treatment of dry orgasm is set as a goal identify the cause and resolve it, if possible. For example, if this happens due to taking an assistant, after stopping its use, the ability to ejaculate is restored. If the cause is stress or a psychological problem, then a consultation with a psychologist can help. In the case of retrograde ejaculation medical treatment is being provided. Dry orgasm, which is a consequence of surgery, can not be cured.

In any case, the treatment of dry orgasm is carried out under the supervision of a doctor.
